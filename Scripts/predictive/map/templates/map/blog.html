Epidemics

One of the biggest threats to international security is pandemics. As a global community, we’re not prepared to respond to outbreaks like Ebola, SARs, Zika, and H1N1, among others. As the world grows ever more interconnected, risk of the rapid spread of outbreaks increases. The international community must work together to build the tools and networks necessary to save millions of lives. Some tools already exist to help us respond to these outbreaks. However, these tools often source information from the internet, meaning we can’t see the spread of an outbreak in places without internet access. This makes it difficult for those responding to outbreaks to make appropriate resource allocation decisions; costing time, money, and lives. More inclusive outbreak mapping tools may be able to paint a more accurate picture of outbreak spread and be used to help respondents make better resource allocation decisions.
