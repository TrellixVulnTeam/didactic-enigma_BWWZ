THE STORY

Governments in developing countries often fail to quickly identify abnormal infectious disease activity because there is no effective tool or information network that allows officials and health workers to regularly share infectious disease information in a timely manner. These governments rely on outdated modes of information sharing, such as paper and other non-electronic reporting methods, making data sharing extremely slow. As a result, national governments are often unable to catch outbreaks before they turn into epidemics. In 2014, it took Guinea nearly three months to detect an outbreak of Ebola. Seventeen months later, the virus had spread to 6 different countries and over 11,000 people had perished.

HealthRAIS continuously monitors infectious diseases in developing countries and quickly detects outbreaks. The web-based platform collects, standardizes, analyzes, and maps data from small clinics and hospitals in these countries using a combination of SMS and internet data. The tool is intended to give governments and NGOs in developing countries a birdâ€™s eye view of infectious disease activity. Users can view disease cases and deaths counts on a map and generate reports at regional, national, state, district, and local levels. HealthRAIS uses clustering and anomaly detection to alert users when abnormal infectious disease activity occurs, enabling them to catch outbreaks in their earliest stages. Armed with this information, governments and NGOs can quickly respond to outbreaks and send resources where they are needed.

Humans will always be susceptible to diseases. Globalization and increasingly fluid borders means that disease can spread father and faster than ever. HealthRAIS combats disease spread by allowing users to see outbreaks as they are beginning so they can respond before thousands or even millions of lives are lost. Future pathogens and outbreaks will come, but by providing consistent information, we can make ourselves less vulnerable to the expedited spread of infectious diseases. Our team holds the unfaltering belief that information in the right hands can be the difference between outbreak and pandemic.

Our Mission

Information in the right hands can make the difference

Orginization and Consistency

More often than not, incoming data is unorganized, inconsistent, and infrequent. Using HealthRAIS, incoming data is aggregated, standardized, and displayed in a uniform way to give the best picture.

Information Network

By recreating the reporting process, HealthRAIS gathers data from local level healthcare providers that wasn't getting reported in the past. With the built-in alert system, users can send mass SMS responses back to numbers registered within the platform.

Early Warning

There is a dire need for accurate, up-to-date information in order to catch outbreaks early on. The ability for a system to detect and show that there is abnormal disease activity occurring creates the opportunity for officials to make the decisions necessary to stop outbreaks in their tracks.

Hard to Reach Places

While there are still parts of the world without internet, HealthRAIS utilizes SMS reporting to gather data from every corner of the region. Now, hospitals and clinics everywhere can report and receive alerts using just one simple cell-phone.
